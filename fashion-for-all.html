Fashion for all!

Michelle K

A mom of 3 boys writing about fashion.  Here's the thing, though I do not agree with caring so much what you wear or how you look that you take away from what is truly important-your heart before God and your motives, I also think being a mom takes a toll in many ways on our bodies.  We as humans think of ourselves with ease, we as moms are no different, however our lives are surrounded by the needs of little ones. 

For me thinking about what I am wearing or putting that cute piece of jewelry on that I love is definitely last on the list.  Making sure every human is clothed, went potty or has a clean diaper, everyone fed and in the car with minimal fussing and crying is success.  Making sure I get all those things done is a miracle.  So, I have found something to help me.

Stitch Fix is a great tool and I found it to actually be fun!  You pay a minimal fee ($20 or less, because they have a lot of deals!), you fill out a styling profile-pick sizes and your style AND price range preference, and a stylist picks an outfit or two for you then it ships straight to your door.  The joys of no mall traffic!  Try it all on, keep what you want and ship back (free of charge) anything you do not want.  If you don't want anything you pay nothing except the stylist charge and if you want one or a few items you pay for those and ship the rest back, if you want it all you get a larger discount and pay online.  

I did it for 3 months straight because I received a coupon for $20 for 3 style fixes.  Now I do it every 3 months which works great for our budget and I let my stylist know if there are any specific wants or upcoming events I'd like items for.  As a mom of 3 young ones getting out and shopping for myself will likely never make it on the priority list.  This way we budget a certain amount and I buy what I like best out of my styling fix sent straight to my door.  They have it available for Men, Women and Kids also.  I have enjoyed it and if you think you would too check it out here and you will get $25, that is your first fix covered!  What do you have to lose?  Try it for free!  Stitch Fix
