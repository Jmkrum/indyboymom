Fluffy, Gluten Free Pancakes

Michelle K

Pancakes used to be my arch nemesis of food, not due to the taste but I could never cook them right.  They would be burnt on the outside and mushy on the inside.  Gross.  So, I accepted my fate as a pancake making failure and that was that.  Until our second son had many food sensitivies and yet one of his favorite treat foods remained...the pancake. 

I tried so many recipes.  No gluten, dairy, refined sugar, or xanthum gum. Right, how am I going to make a good tasting pancake without these ingredients?  No box mix would work, mainly because of the xanthum gum and also because I could not bring myself to pay that much for something I could surely figure out on my own.  After many and I do mean many a failed recipe we got it!  We found the successful pancake recipe with no gluten, dairy, refined sugar or xanthum gum.  And I am sharing the secret with you, they are light and fluffy and truly are delicious.  My husband actually says there will not be a better pancake and of course, my boys devour them.  Hope you too can enjoy this recipe with cleaner ingredients than most.

Fluffy, Gluten-free Pancakes

  • 2 cups flour (we use King Arthur's gluten free flour-no xanthum gum, if there is no xanthum gum sensitivity I am sure the regular King Arthur gluten free flour would work.)
  • 3 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 Tablespoons raw honey
  • 1 1/4 cups milk (we use unsweetened vanilla almond milk, but any non dairy milk will probably do).
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 tsp vanilla extract

Combine flour, baking soda and salt in a medium bowl.  In a separate bowl beat egg then add milk, vanilla, honey and stir vigorously.  Once mixed well add liquid to the medium bowl of flour and mix.  Some small clumps are okay, but there should not be a lot.  

Heat non stick skillet low-medium heat.  If pancake sticks use a small amount of butter-we use avocado butter or coconut oil to grease the skillet.  Pour about a 1/4 cup into skillet and wait for it to bubble then flip.  Once it is golden brown on both sides your pancake is ready.  This recipe makes about 6-8 medium pancakes.
