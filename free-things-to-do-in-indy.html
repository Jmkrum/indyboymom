Free or Inexpensive Things to do in Indy

Michelle K

If you live in Indiana you know the drill, wait 10 minutes and the weather will be different.  So, Spring is sometimes in the air currently in Indy!  At least spring break is right around the corner for most Indianpolis area schools, so I thought I would compile a short list of some fun places to visit in Indy on a tight budget.  We try to include as much education as possible so tagging on science and history to a family outing is easy and fun, even if you are on spring break!

Indiana War Memorial-This is a beautiful building and admission is FREE.  It is a great museum, enjoyed by even the younger kids.  The helicopter is always the highlight for my boys.  There is easy street parking available at a minimal cost and with the museum being free it is worth the price.  There is a nice park in front of the memorial also and the Central Library is walking distance too.  

Canal walk-The canal is always a beautiful place to walk around, there are a few memorials you can check out on or right by the canal.  The 9/11 Memorial, Medal of Honor Memorial (beautiful at night), and the USS Indianapolis Memorial.  There is also a newer playground called the Colts Canal Playscape that is a great place for the kids to burn some energy.

Newfields-the art museum itself does cost, but if it is a nice day take full advantage of the beautiful outdoor acres.  There is a garden and 100 acres of nature with artwork throughout.  Areas to play, picnic and a pretty lake to enjoy.

Garfield Park Conservatory-this is a favorite for us around Christmas because they have trains and beautiful lights throughout the greenhouse area.  There is no charge, but they do ask for donation.  This is a nice getaway to be in nature in Indy when it is 15° outside in winter or enjoy the outdoor area also on warmer days.

Eagle Creek Park-Admission is $5 per vehicle for in county and $6 for out of county residents.  This park is beautiful and is one of the top 10 largest municipal parks in the country. They have hiking, boating, fishing and playgrounds just to name a few activites.

Fort Harrison State Park-This is our go to park on those beautiful, sunny Indiana days!  We love the creeks, hiking trails and the big slide is a favorite for our boys.  Fort Harrison also has horse rides available.  Admission is $7 for in state residents and $9 for out of state.
