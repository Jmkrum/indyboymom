Always a Time of Thanks

Michelle K

I was reading a book (Thanksgiving on Plymouth Plantation by Diane Stanley) with my kids recently and learned that it was Abraham Lincoln who made the last Thursday in November the official Thanksgiving holiday.  It was also not set as a time to remember the pilgrims and indians having a time of thanks together; that is called a fall festival which began hundreds of years prior to Thanksgiving Day and was an annual celebration feast after the harvest between the pilgrims and indians.  

So, the actual Thanksgiving Day.  A day established in America during the middle of the Civil War.  A time to set aside in the midst of a war torn, divided country to give thanks to God for His faithfulness and blessings.  Seems ironic, huh?  To give thanks and think on blessings when not much is "going right".  Perhaps we should do the same though.  2020 has been a difficult year for most.  But if we take time out to remember all the good things that happened, the laughter even if it was over Zoom or Facetime and the blessings even of technology perhaps we can begin to agree with Abraham Lincoln, in a time of tumult there really is still a lot to be grateful for.  

Ideas to spur on thankfulness with your family:

  • A thankfulness tree:  Grab a branch from the backyard and put it in a cup with rocks or something heavy to keep it in.  Then take scrap pieces of paper (if you're crafty cut them into leaves) and each day have each person write down what they are thankful for.
  • A time of thanks, each night at dinner go around the table and ask each person what they are thankful for that day.
  • Keep a thankfulness journal personally or as a family or better yet, both!  Write down what you're thankful for each day and be sure to look back at how faithful God has been and how many things we can give thanks for in all times.  
  • Go on a prayer of thanks walk, thank God for different things your entire walk.
  • Look up scripture references that include thanks and read them as a family.

Cultivating a heart of gratitude is important in our lives and important to help guide our children toward also.  Have you ever spent time with someone who has "the whole world against them" at all times?  It doesn't matter what is going on in their lives, words of thanks are rarely uttered.  On the contrary I am not saying pretend everything is okay when it is a difficult season, however, I fully agree with President Lincoln that it is always a time of thanks.  There are always things to be grateful for, we just need to train our eyes and hearts to see them more often.
