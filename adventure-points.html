Adventure Points

Michelle K

Are you tired of your kids saying they're bored?  Do you struggle with having too much screen time at your house?  Do you wish your kids were more active, especially during summer break?  I felt the same way.  When we aren't in the normal school routine I get lazy just as fast as they do it seems!  So, I had an idea and created something I called our Summer Adventure Points.  

The kids had to complete so many tasks worth so many points before they could have screen time.  It also helped encourage siblings to actually get along, play together and DO something not just sit in front of a screen or tell me they're bored or stand in front of the fridge saying, "I'm hungry, can we eat lunch now?" At 8am exactly 10 minutes after they ate breakfast.  If they came to me with a request to watch something or play on the Ipad or if they dared to complain about being bored I simply sent them to the Summer Adventure board and told them to pick something and do it.  It ended up being a fun way to spend time together even on the dreaded rainy days.  

Here is a free Adventure Point Sheet you can print out for your family.  If you want to tweak it to suite your family more I actually wrote ours out on a poster board and hung it up in the entryway.  We kept track daily on our calendar and then weekly there is a section to write in your total weekly points.  We did ours for 3 months so 500 points was a  challenge to some, but definitely attainable!
