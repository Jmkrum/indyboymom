Dear first time mom, Part 2

Michelle K

Dear first time mom, (Letter 2)

Are you feeling inadequate?  Worn out?  Like you can't remember when you last showered, slept or maybe you just napped in the shower?  This job of motherhood is HARD.  The only perfect parents are those without kids.  Right?  Remember when you were single and you sat at a restaurant rolling your eyes, oh so subtly, at the screaming baby or tantrum throwing toddler in the next booth?  And those words you dared to speak, "When I have kids they definitely won't act like that."  Don't worry we were all that foolish at one point and then we had our first child.  Turns out this parenting thing is crazy hard and impossible without the grace of God.

Watch out for the sin trap of comparing.  Comparison will rob you of the joy of motherhood.  Don't look at every other moms posts and pictures and compare yourself and your child.  I think every time we do that and think she has it all together or I wish I had that we are saying to an almighty, all knowing, wise God, "You didn't do this right."  Or "I need something else besides what You have given me."  God gave your child to you and His ways are perfect, beyond our understanding.  He placed this child in your arms for a specific reason.  He has given you and your husband the task of raising this child in the way they should go.  Rest in Him, trust Him, follow Him and seek hard after Him.  There's nothing better you could do for your child.

With my first son I struggled a lot with devotions or what my time with God was to look like in that season of life.  I thought I still needed to be having half hour to an hour long time in the Word.  If I ever had a half hour sitting in that season of life I was asleep in the first 5 minutes for sure!  Your time with God will change, it is inevitable.  It is also imperitive that you still have time with God.  I personally think it looks different for each mom in each season.  God has always cared about our soul, our motives.  If we are genuinely seeking Him, wanting to know Him more and humbly desiring to obey Him I think He is honored whether we are in the Word for 10 minutes or 60 minutes.    

A few tips to help in the ever changing seasons of having a little one:

  • Listen to the Bible on audio. 
  • Keep a Bible next to where you nurse or often sit with the baby.
  • Listen to solid sermons or podcasts, rich in the Word and truth.  Some examples:
    • If your church has their sermons online relisten to the one you just heard.  Chances are you will catch something new or simply benefit from hearing it again.
    • Look at the Book from DesiringGod.com
    • John Piper sermons
    • Heidi St John podcast
    • Risen Motherhood podcast

Every season will be different, but lean into the Lord, always seek Him and seek to know the God of the Bible more.  I think asking questions as you study God's Word is helpful even if you only ask what does this Scripture teach me about who God is?  Enjoy Him and enjoy the little blessing He gave you.  

For His glory alone,

Michelle K
