Homemade Hot Cocoa

Michelle K

Grey clouds cover the sky, white flakes are falling and the temperature is brutal at 11° farenheit.  The sting of winter is at its peak on this late January day in Indiana.  We have a commin hoosier joke that says if you don't like the weather in Indiana wait 10 minutes.  It really was 55°F just last week.  I love Indiana and the seasons we are able to enjoy.  My boys love Indiana weather also, it's not every kid who can ride their bike on Sunday and by Tuesday afternoon be enjoying a snowball fight with the neighbors.  I

I love when they try to go running out of the house only to be stopped by me the winter wear police making sure they actually are sufficiently dressed to play in the snow.  I usually make myself some hot peppermint tea and sit at the kitchen table looking out as they build a snowman or throw snowballs at each other.  I always keep the window cracked so they hear me remind them no head shots, boys.  And when their little noses get so red they can no longer bear, I get up and begin the most important task.  Making the hot cocoa for them to have as soon as they unload their cold, wet clothes into the laundry basket.  

They come in slowly from the cold and wrap warm blankets around their little bodies and plop right down on the heat register in the kitchen to thaw out.  When everyone is in I serve them their hot cocoa and watch as they take their first sip.  They wrap their little fingers around their mug to embrace the warmth and a smile arises across each face as their tastebuds enjoy every ounce of the warm chocolate.

If you're used to a simple packet of hot cocoa I urge you to try this recipe.  It really is not hard at all.  The small amount of time it takes to make your hot cocoa is worth the extra MAYBE 5 minutes vs a packet of hot cocoa in every sip!  You won't regret it.  This recipe takes me maybe 10 minutes on the stove top.  

 

Homemade Hot Cocoa-for 1 serving-double or triple recipe for more people

2 cups of milk (we use non dairy-vanilla almond milk)

1 Tablespoon of honey

1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract

3 tablespoons of raw cocoa powder (we buy this in bulk when it is on sale at Fresh Thyme, and stock up for the winter.  You can get it in smaller batches in the bakery aisle at your grocery store or buy it in bulk online).

Heat milk on low-medium heat, add cocoa powder and honey then vanilla extract.  Stir and heat until temperature you prefer.  Enjoy!
