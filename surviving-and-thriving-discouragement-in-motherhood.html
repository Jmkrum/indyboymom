Surviving and Thriving: Discouragement in Motherhood

Michelle K

Recently I was talking with a friend who is in the thick of mothering little ones.  For me when we had 2 under 2 and 3 years old it was hard.  Very hard for me.  In fact, I am not joking that I was convinced someone would not survive and the most likely victim would be me.  I wish I had great encouraging advice that made my friends circumstances and the hardness of parenting the little years a bit lighter for her, but honestly, I had nothing to offer her.  

I remember at that same season of life hearing a speaker at a moms group and the topic was not just surviving but thriving in motherhood.  At the end of it I felt inspirired, but I would say the inspiration quickly wore off as soon as I went to pick up my son from the nursery and tried to buckle his defiant self into the carseat.  As I look back on motherhood thus far there are few times I would say there is a lot of thriving going on.  I think we are so prone to discouragement when we don't feel like we're thriving as moms.

As moms we feel like we are living in the movie Groundhog Day...forever.  Somehow the seasons change, but my boys are still doing the exact thing I could have sworn I disciplined them for yesterday, and the day before that and before that.  

Perhaps you're in that boat.  Maybe it isn't the exact same circumstances, but maybe you can relate a little or a lot.  Maybe you're the mom holding a newborn and feeling the exhaustion of constant feedings and diaper changes and wardrobe changes because of the spit up or diaper malfunction.  Perhaps you're the mom with the toddler who has found he can now speak your language and enjoys yelling, "no, mommy!".  Or perhaps you're the mom of the teenager who is struggling at school and your own heart is breaking as you watch your child struggle in battle.  Whatever your season, whatever your circumstancial struggle is, we have all been there: discouragement.  

We've lost sight in the valley of discouragement of the final destination-the mountain top, and that is okay.  I don't believe discouragement is a sin.  Perhaps when we are discouraged we are more apt to sin in various ways (gluttony, laziness, anger, or a myriad of selfishness), but to be discouraged is not a sin.  In our times of discouragement it is an opportunity.  An opportunity for faithfulness.  Faithful obedience to the next thing.  The next thing may not be glamorous, the next thing may not and probably will not provide relief.  But if we are faithful by His grace to obey Him and to do the next thing He has called us to do: get up and feed the baby, discipline the toddler in love again, ask for forgiveness again, pray over your child again; well, this is how we walk out of discouragement.  Not over night usually, but step by step.  I hope I can look back at the times of discouragment in motherhood and say by God's grace we didn't JUST survive it, and I highly doubt there will be many times I feel like I thrived in it, but I hope to be faithful through it.
