Just a Moment

Michelle K

I had one of those moments recently.  You know the kind, baby is on the tabletop like a ninja eating the markers as the noodles boil over and the 5 year old was throwing a fit because he suddenly hates the noddles he loved yesterday and asked for 30 minutes ago, while the 6 year old gives me a complete blank stare as I give a seemingly pointless lecture for the umpteenth time about putting his markers away and away does not mean the center of the kitchen table.  I glance at the clock and see the numbers laughing at me saying you still have 2 hours until your husband is home! 

Yep, it was one of those moments.  The kind you just want to curl up in a ball and pull your covers over your head and hope everything is in order and your children are miraculously thankful, obedient humans when you come back out.  But alas, you're a mom and that is not an option.  These humans depend on you, not to mention you cannot leave the boiling over noodles=fire hazard.  

In these moments, I struggle.  I am pretty sure I am going to lose my sanity every time these moments come, but so far everything seems fairly in tact.  The greatest thing about these moments in the grand scheme of motherhood is they are just moments.  They do not last.  Some may be longer than others and yes, we have had hard seasons of parenting, but again we have come through it in the end.  I hope my boys will know in the struggle and chaotic moments of life there is deep grace.  These times are no different than any other at the heart of it; we seek the Lord, we pray a lot, we lean hard into the truths of God's Word and at least I can know my boys will know what a true apology is, because most of the time I am having to apologize for poorly handling the chaotic moments. 

I would be sorely remissed to sit there though, to stay in the struggle.  Once it is done, it is done.  Calm comes again or some simulance of it anyway.  There are moments of struggle and chaos every day it seems in motherhood, but when we lay our head on the pillow at night I hope much more often than not we are filled with memories of the moments of laughter, snuggling reading a book, those sleepy brown eyes that gave me one more glance as he says I love you, Mommy. 

So, sweet mommas, we all have those moments.  Moments of insanity it feels like, utter chaos abounding, but lets remember they are just moments, they will pass one way or another.  Even the long seasons will eventually pass and one day when I am older and gray I hope the moments me and my boys have stored away the most are moments of great grace and joy.
