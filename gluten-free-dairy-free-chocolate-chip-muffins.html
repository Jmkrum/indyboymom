Gluten Free, Dairy Free Chocolate Chip Muffins

Michelle K

It is hard to find a good muffin that is gluten free that isn't overpowered by coconut or almond flour, in my opinion.  We have found a gluten free flour that works well as a substitute for recipes that call for flour and we always substitute honey for the sugar.  So, with some tweaking I finally found a good consistency in these chocolate chip muffins that the boys love.

We also like these because they only make about 10 muffins for us which between 5 of us is reasonable and doesn't leave any leftovers for us to overeat on.  These can be kept in a tupperware or ziploc bag for a few days or can be frozen also.  Hope you enjoy!

 

Chocolate Chip Muffins 

Ingredients

1 1/2 cups gluten free all purpose flour-we use a brand at our local grocery called King Arthur, but as long as it is gluten free and all purpose, it should work fine.

1/4 cup or less honey

2 teaspoons baking powder

1/4 teaspoon salt

1 egg

1 1/4 cup milk-we use rice milk, but any non dairy milk should work.

1/4 cup oil

1 teaspoon vanilla

1 cup chocolate chips-we use the brand Enjoy Life

Directions:

In a medium mixing bowl beat the egg.  Add the milk, oil, vanilla and honey and mix well.  In a larger bowl add flour, baking powder and salt and wisk together.  Add the wet ingredients from the medium bowl to the larger bowl and wisk well until smooth with few or no lumps.  Fold in chocolate chips.  Pour into a muffin tin and bake at 350 for 10-12 minutes or until center is not gooey.
