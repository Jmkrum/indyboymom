Ideas to focus on others while quarantining

Michelle K

We are definitely living in a different world than we were just a few weeks ago.  There is not a person unaffected, so during these challenging times we can get bitter or better and I choose better.  I exhort you to set an example to your kids in these times, not an example of perfection but an example of choosing to be a blessing where you can, being patient and enduring with grace and love.    

I know a lot of moms or dads or grandparents are home with kids right now unexpectedly and for God only knows how long, so I wanted to offer some great tips of things to do during this time.

It's our nature to be focused on ourselves and a time like this is no exception and is a time where you see that selfishness even more.  So, here are 6 things to do with your kids to focus on others!

6 ideas to focus on others while quarantining!

  1. Write a letter or draw a picture for:
    • first responders
    • local firehouse
    • local police station
    • local courthouse/government
    • military/service men and women either those you personally know or go here.
    • family/friends you currently cannot visit
    • pediatrician/family doctor and nurses
    • local hospital or nursing home staff
    • pastors/pastoral staff
    • teachers/school staff
    • someone you know who is in the hospital or nursing home or go here.
    • local grocery store-have you seen these peoples faces recently?  I am pretty sure a simple thank you and a card from a child thanking them would make their day pretty easily!
    • think of people in your area that may be feeling the impact in a different and possibly more difficult way than you and your family and encourage them with a letter of thanks.
  2. Pray for:
    • our government
    • those who have lost someone to this virus
    • doctors and nurses
    • those working to develop and find a cure or vaccine
  3. Anonymously bless a neighbor with something they may need: some money in an envelope, some diapers left on their porch, some toilet paper for goodness sakes!  Make it a little care package-put some kleenex, hand sanitizer, a nice note in it.
  4. Do chores for each other-each person come up with something (within reason) that would be helpful to another family member and do it.
  5. Make a craft for someone your child loves: neighbors, grandparents, parents, etc.  
  6. CALL a friend.  Calling someone is when your cell phone actually goes up to your ear and you talk to the person, you can even hear their voice.  It's a completely lost art nowadays, we don't know our phones can be used for talking besides talk text.  Call a different friend each day to see how they are doing.  Try it, I think you'll like it!
