Cookie Baking With Little Ones

Michelle K

This recipe is near and dear to my heart.  Nothing fancy at all, very simple and actually has some great fine motor skill building in it with the unwrapping of the candy.  This is a recipe I would help my grandma make every year and they were my favorite.  This may be my first totally unhealthy recipe, but alas once a year having a few and sharing with friends is enjoyable, (pending no peanut allergies)!  This recipe is SO easy to do and I highly suggest doing it with kids 2 and up.  Even the 2 year olds can unwrap the candies and place the dough in the tin and they LOVE being helpers in the kitchen and having a part in the baking process.  

Peanut Butter Hershey Kiss And Reese's Peanut Butter Cup Cookies

  • Peanut butter cookie dough-you can make your own or buy a prepackaged variety.  I like the plan peanut butter dough, but there are different varieties, like chocolate chip, etc. We use the 16.5 ounce roll, but you could also buy the break apart kind in 16.5 ounce.
  • Hershey Kisses about a pound size bag
  • Miniature Reese's Peanut Butter Cup about a pound size bag

Directions:

Place 1 x 1 inch cubes in a mini muffin tin and bake at 350 for 9-11 minutes.  While dough is baking unwrap kisses and cups.  Let cookies cool for 1 minute then carefully place kisses and cups in center of cookies.  Let cool completely and then remove from tin.  The entire 16.5 ounce dough makes about 24 cookies.
