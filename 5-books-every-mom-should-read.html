5 Books Every Mom Should Read

Michelle K

We love reading!  There are so many benefits to reading individually and reading to your children.  My husband caught my attention before we started dating with his really cute dimple smile and also we were reading the same book.  It was even a Tozer book, so I knew this guy was different and his interest in deep theology attracted me to him.  If you don't like reading, audiobooks are a great option.  I always kept a book, a Bible or both in the places I would nurse the baby most often, even if I only got a few verses read or a few pages, it was something and it was helpful.  

So, without further ado here are 5 books I think every mom should read, in no particular order!  

  1. Risen Motherhood by Emily Jensen and Laura Wifler
    • This book is solid in bringing the Gospel to the realities of motherhood.  No longer does Jesus seem like a distant need that all moms have, instead this book brings the depth of my desperate need for a Savior and Lord into everyday mothering.
  2. Mom Enough by Rachel Jankovic, Gloria Furman, Rachel Pieh Jones, Christine Hoover, Carolyn McCulley, Trillia Newbell, Christina Fox 
    • This book is actually short articles from DesiringGod.com compiled into one book.  It is rich with truths to encourage and short enough even moms can get through the chapters and book fairly quick.
  3. Becoming Mom Strong by Heidi St. John
    • I was a heap of tears reading the Epilogue of this book, it is something I reread frequently just to gain perspective.  I respect Heidi St. John and her ministry.  She really seeks to keep the Bible at the forefront of everything she does and always seeks to encourage parents in this battle of parenthood. 
  4. Loving the Little Years by Rachel Jankovic
    • This book is another short one easy enough for moms to get through actually.  Rachel does a great job in this book of helping mommas of little ones see the joys and struggles as gifts from God.  This book helps me gain perspective in these oh so tiring years with little ones.
  5. The Read-Aloud Family by Sarah Mackenzie
    • As I said we love reading as a family and this book gives great insight on research that has been done showing many ways reading aloud is beneficial for kids and families.  I think this is a great book for parents, grandparents, aunts, uncles, anyone who has a child in their life.

Happy reading, mommas, enjoy!
