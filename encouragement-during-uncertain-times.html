Encouragement during uncertain times

Michelle K

Fear, worry, stress, anxiety, job loss, financial strain and unparalleled health concerns.  Where are we going to turn when the stress comes?  I think this is a time for believers to rise up, this can be a time of revival.  A time that we lean into the Lord like never before and trust Him more deeply, because He is always trustworthy.

I am a certified Biblical counselor through ACBC and here are some practical tips for combatting fear and anxiety I would offer during these uncertain times.

  1. Think, Act, Feel.  We innately feel, act and then think, but if we are constantly acting off of our feelings-fear, worry, stress then we are not acting in a way that is honoring to God.  First we need to think rightly-ask God to renew our minds-help us to put off the wrong thoughts and sinful desires and put on what honors Him.  Ephesians 4:22-24
  2. Memorize scripture: proclaiming the truth even to yourself is powerful.  If we are going to fill our minds with the latest on the coronavirus we will be consumed with thoughts about the coronavirus.  If we choose to fill our minds with the truth of God's Word we can be consumed with thoughts of truth and peace no matter what the circumstances.  Here is a short list of some Scriptures to memorize regarding fear/worry:  
    • Romans 8:15
    • 2 Timothy 1:7
    • Hebrews 13:5-6
    • Psalm 27:1
    • Psalm 56:3-4, 10-11 
    • 1 John 4:18
    • Philippians 4:4-8
  3. Read the Bible and pray.  Turn off the tv/internet/phone and read your Bible and pray.  2 great books for prayer is A Simply Way to Pray by Martin Luther, it's a small book that teaches you how to pray.  Also, a great book of Puritan prayers called The Valley of Vision
  4. Read this book I recommend to almost every single counselee and I have read more times that I can count is Trusting God by Jerry Bridges.  The root of our worry and fear and anxiety is that we are not at rest and trusting in God, which we all struggle with at times.
  5. Lastly, here is a link to an incredibly encouraging post by Tim Challis about worship taking place around the globe.  It may look different everywhere, but God is being worshiped and that is encouraging!!
